Chhattisgarh, located in central India, faces challenges related to air quality, especially in its urban centers and industrial areas. Despite its rich natural resources and significant industrial development, Chhattisgarh experiences air pollution due to various factors. Factors Affecting Air Quality: 1. Industrial Activities: Chhattisgarh's industrial regions, including major cities like Raipur and Bhilai, contribute to air pollution through emissions of pollutants such as sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and particulate matter (PM). 2. Mining Activities: The mining industry in Chhattisgarh, particularly for coal and other minerals, can lead to air pollution through dust and pollutant emissions. 3. Biomass Burning: Agricultural practices, including crop residue burning, contribute to air pollution during certain seasons, especially in rural areas. 4. Urbanization: Rapid urbanization and construction activities in Chhattisgarh's urban centers contribute to dust and pollutant emissions, impacting air quality. Health Impacts: Poor air quality in Chhattisgarh can have adverse health effects on its residents. Exposure to high levels of air pollution can lead to respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, and other health issues. Vulnerable groups such as children, the elderly, and individuals with pre-existing health conditions are particularly at risk.
