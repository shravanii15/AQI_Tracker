About Gujarat

Over the past ten years, Gujarat has faced significant challenges regarding air quality, influenced by a combination of factors including industrial activities, agricultural practices, vehicular emissions, and meteorological conditions. The state's major cities and industrial hubs, including Ahmedabad, Surat, Vadodara, and Rajkot, have experienced varying levels of air pollution, particularly during certain seasons and weather conditions. Industrial emissions, vehicular traffic, construction activities, and geographical factors contribute to air quality issues in the region. Additionally, agricultural activities such as crop residue burning, though less prevalent compared to other states, also contribute to localized air pollution. Efforts to address air pollution in Gujarat have included measures to control industrial emissions, promote cleaner technologies, improve public transportation, and raise awareness about the importance of air quality. Despite these efforts, continued vigilance and collaborative action are necessary to mitigate air pollution and protect public health and the environment in Gujarat.
